**[Open Claude Code on screen. Show the clean interface.]**

"I'm using Claude Code‚ÄîAnthropic's desktop app. It's free if you have a Claude account. You can also do this in the browser at claude.ai. I prefer the desktop app because it's faster and doesn't eat my browser tabs."

"I'm creating a new project. I'll call it 'Speaker Packet Agent.'"

"Here's the scenario: You run events. Every event has speakers. Every speaker sends you a bio, a headshot, sometimes a novel, sometimes chaos. ## STEP 3: GIVE THE AGENT ITS INSTRUCTIONS (2 min)

**[Switch to your text editor with the Speaker_Content_Processor_Instructions.txt file]**

"Now I'm going to give this agent its job description. This is the only 'code' we're writing‚Äîand it's just plain English instructions."

**[Copy the full instruction set from the text file]**

**[Paste it into Claude Code chat]**

**[On screen, the instructions appear. Scroll slowly so they can see the structure but don't read every word aloud. Highlight key sections:]**

"Look at what I'm telling it to do:

1. Rewrite the bio into three formats: 50 words, 100 words, 1-sentence intro.
2. Rewrite the session description into a 75-word abstract with three key takeaways.
3. Generate alt text for the headshot for accessibility.
4. Create a quality control checklist‚Äîflag missing tech requirements, vague descriptions, name mismatches.
5. Export everything in a table I can paste straight into Cvent or Swoogo."

**[Scroll down to the BANNED WORDS section]**

"And here's my favorite part: I gave it a banned words list. 'Synergy,' 'thought leader,' 'disrupt,' 'leverage'‚Äîall the corporate buzzwords that make attendees' eyes glaze over. If the original bio has those words, the agent rewrites them into something specific and concrete instead."

**[Pause.]**

"That's it. That's the whole instruction set. No coding. No API keys. Just clear English telling the AI exactly what good work looks like."

**[Hit Enter. Show the agent starting to process.]**

"Now watch."

## STEP 4: SHOW THE OUTPUT (3 min)

**[Agent processes. Output appears on screen. Read key sections aloud as they appear:]**

**[When the 50-word bio appears:]**

"Look at this. 50-word bio: 'Jane Smith is a customer experience strategist who has led transformation projects for Fortune 500 companies including Microsoft and Adobe. She specializes in turning complex feedback into actionable roadmaps. Jane lives in Portland with two rescue dogs and an impressive collection of event badges.'

Professional. Specific. No buzzwords. One personal detail that makes her human."

**[When the 1-sentence intro appears:]**

"Emcee intro: 'Please welcome Jane Smith, a customer experience strategist who's helped companies like Microsoft turn messy feedback into winning strategies.'

That's speakable. You can hand that to your emcee and they won't stumble over it."

**[When the session abstract appears:]**

"Session abstract: 'In this session, you'll learn how to transform vague attendee feedback into clear, actionable improvements for your next event. "Alt text for accessibility: 'Professional headshot of Jane Smith, a woman with short brown hair wearing a navy blazer, smiling at the camera against a neutral gray background.'

## TECH TROUBLESHOOTING CHECKLIST

**If Claude Code is slow:**
- "The agent is thinking through 300 words of messy bio and rewriting it into three clean formats. This takes about 30 seconds."
- Keep talking, fill the silence with narration

**If output has errors:**
- "Interesting‚Äîit kept one buzzword. Let me show you how to refine the instructions..."
- Turn it into a teaching moment about iteration

**If upload fails:**
- "Let me try a different file format..." (have a backup PDF version ready)

**If internet drops:**
- Immediately pivot to backup video: "This is why we always have a Plan B..."
